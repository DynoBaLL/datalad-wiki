# About

## Project Background

This website is the result of a project initiated by the [The Dynamics of Brain and Language Lab](https://dynoball.org/) at the University of Geneva. 
It received a grant from the University of Geneva's [Open Research Data](https://www.unige.ch/researchdata/en/presentation/news/call-ord-2025-funded-projects/)
funding program, which supports initiatives aimed at improving the management and sharing of research data.



## Aims
Working collaboratively on research data is still difficult. Even with shared services such as {term}`NAS`, collaborative workflows often create additional costs in time, money, or both. The coexistence of multiple infrastructures, including {term}`HPC` systems, {term}`NAS`, local storage, and virtual machines, frequently leads research groups to maintain several copies of the same data across different environments. Beyond being inefficient, this duplication makes data traceability, organization, and sharing more difficult.

This project aims to provide a practical wiki for using {term}`datalad` within the University of Geneva's infrastructure in order to reduce these costs and simplify day-to-day data management. By making it easier to manage, track, and share data across institutional services such as {term}`NAS` and {term}`S3`, we hope to help researchers spend less time handling avoidable logistical issues and more time focusing on what matters most: data quality, reproducibility, and effective sharing.
